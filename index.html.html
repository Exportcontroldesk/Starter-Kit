🔥 The Chapter Most Guides Skip
The 600 Series: Why Everything Changed in 2013 — and Why Most Companies Still Don't Know It
Export Control Reform moved thousands of defense items from ITAR to a new class of EAR controls: the 600-series ECCNs. Same military pedigree. Different licensing pathway. De minimis rules now apply. SNAP-R instead of D-Trade. If your company makes parts, components, or electronics with any defense application, and you haven't done a 600-series analysis, you may be over-complying with ITAR on items that have already been released — or under-complying on items that still require strict EAR controls. Chapter 4 covers every 600-series category, the catch-and-release test, and when the result changes your compliance program.

SaaS / Cloud: Is Remote Login an Export?

Hardware: EU Manufacturer Ships to Asia
European plant. U.S.-origin microprocessors inside. Customer in Asia. Nobody touches U.S. soil — but U.S. re-export controls may apply anyway. We work through the de minimis calculation, the Foreign Direct Product Rule, and the Country Chart analysis that determines whether a BIS license is needed before the container leaves Rotterdam.
📐 Scenario 3
Engineering Data: CAD File to a Subcontractor
Your engineer sends a 3D model by email. The subcontractor's technical contact is a foreign national. That email may be a deemed export of ITAR technical data — even if it was just for a quote. We cover how to classify the data (not just the part), when a TAA is required, and what PLM/PDM access controls your TCP must include.
